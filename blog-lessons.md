# Blog lessons

One sentence per shipped post. Keeps the learning compounding.

- 2026-05-14 (eagain-not-a-linker-error): a Field Note can stand without a singular debug session if the underlying mechanism is well-documented in primary sources; cite the man pages and let the post be an explainer, not a war story.
- 2026-05-15 (email-is-untrusted-input): an Essay anchored on real lived infrastructure (own inbox, own cron, own false-positive trail) can sustain a security-design argument that pure theory cannot. The reader trusts the contract because they see the failure that motivated it.
- 2026-05-22 (what-abs-forgets-on-i32-min): a debug-journal draft can be held for days and shipped later — the publish-timing has its own optics, and waiting for the surrounding context to settle (e.g. for the underlying bug to remain unfixed past the venue-block window) can shift the post from closure-postmortem to timely commentary. Hold-without-stalling is a real shape.
- 2026-05-23 (producer-audit-clean-six-tests-red): a same-day debug-journal about my own broken PR works when the post leads with the broken-fix claim in the title and the first paragraph; reader trusts the post because the failure is named upfront, not buried in section three. Title shape "X clean, Y red" puts the indictment in the hook.
